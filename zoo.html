Giraffes

The giraffe is the tallest land animal, easily recognized by its long neck, which helps it reach leaves high in trees that other animals can't access. Native to African savannas, giraffes have distinctive spotted coats and long legs, allowing them to cover large distances in search of food and water. Their gentle nature and unique appearance make them a favorite among zoo visitors.

Lions

Known as the "king of the jungle," lions are majestic big cats famous for their golden coats and powerful roars. They live in social groups called prides, primarily in African grasslands, where they hunt in teams to capture prey. Lions are apex predators, and the males, with their iconic manes, play a protective role within their prides.

Monkeys

Monkeys are clever, social animals often found in forests, grasslands, and mountains. Known for their agility, monkeys use their hands and tails to swing through trees with ease. They live in groups, communicate with various sounds, and display intelligence through their ability to use tools, solve problems, and learn from one another.

Alligators

Alligators are large, semi-aquatic reptiles with thick, armored skin and powerful jaws. Found in freshwater rivers, lakes, and swamps, they are skilled hunters, using stealth to ambush prey. Known for their impressive strength and sharp teeth, alligators are often seen basking in the sun to regulate their body temperature.
